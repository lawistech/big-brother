Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to our most commonly asked questions about moving services.

How far in advance should I book my move?

We recommend booking your move at least 2-4 weeks in advance, especially during peak moving season (May-September). For moves during these busier months, booking 4-6 weeks ahead is ideal to secure your preferred date.

Are you licensed and insured?

Yes, Big Brother Moving is fully licensed and insured. Our comprehensive insurance coverage protects your belongings during the entire moving process, giving you peace of mind throughout your relocation.

Do you provide packing supplies?

Yes, we offer a complete range of packing supplies including boxes, bubble wrap, packing paper, tape, and specialty containers for delicate items. We can provide these materials as part of our full-service packing or make them available for purchase if you prefer to pack yourself.

How do you handle fragile or valuable items?

Our professional movers are trained in proper techniques for handling fragile and valuable items. We use specialized packing materials and methods to ensure maximum protection. For particularly valuable items, we recommend discussing them specifically during your estimate so we can take extra precautions.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept cash, credit cards (Visa, MasterCard, American Express, Discover), debit cards, and certified checks. A deposit may be required to secure your moving date, with the balance due upon completion of your move.

Expert Moving Tips

Make your move easier with these professional tips from our experienced team.

Create a Moving Timeline

Start planning 8 weeks before your move. Create a week-by-week checklist to stay organized and ensure nothing is forgotten.

Take Inventory

Document all your belongings with photos and create a detailed inventory. This helps with insurance and ensures nothing gets lost during the move.

Schedule Utility Transfers

Contact utility companies at least two weeks in advance to schedule disconnection at your current home and connection at your new residence.

Pack Room by Room

Pack one room at a time and clearly label boxes with contents and destination room. Color coding can help for quick identification.

Pack an Essentials Box

Pack a separate box with essentials you'll need immediately upon arrival: toiletries, medications, important documents, and a change of clothes.

Protect Fragile Items

Wrap fragile items individually in packing paper and use bubble wrap for extra protection. Fill empty spaces in boxes to prevent shifting.

Prepare Your Home

Cover floors with protective materials and remove doors if needed to facilitate furniture movement. Clear pathways for movers.

Plan for Children and Pets

Arrange for children and pets to be away from the home during loading and unloading to ensure their safety and reduce stress.

Keep Important Items With You

Carry valuables, important documents, and irreplaceable items with you rather than loading them onto the moving truck.

Set Up Bedrooms First

Make beds and unpack bedroom essentials first so everyone has a comfortable place to sleep after a long moving day.

Inspect Your New Home

Check for any issues like water leaks, electrical problems, or HVAC issues upon arrival. Document any existing damage.

Locate Important Community Features

Identify nearby emergency services, grocery stores, pharmacies, and other essential locations in your new neighborhood.
